Projects
Tackling Multiple Visual Artifacts: Blind Image Restoration using Conditional Adversarial Networks (ongoing)
V Jeya Maria Jose, M Anand, A Ashwin Natraj, K Subramanian, S Deivalakshmi

Restoring images that are degraded by multiple visual artifacts like noise, blurness and other environmental visual artifacts like shadow, snow, rain and haze is a challenging task. In this work, use of conditional adversarial networks for this task has been explored. ISTD dataset augmented with different types of noise and blurness has been used to train the network.

Cuff-less Blood Pressure Prediction using Convolutional and Long Short-term Memory Networks from ECG and PPG signals (ongoing)
V Jeya Maria Jose, M Anand, Geerthy T, M Siddarth, K Subramanian, G Uma

Existing methods for cuff-less blood pressure (BP) prediction deal with extracting various features manually from electrophysiological signals like Electrocardiogram and Photoplethysmogram and then using analytical or machine learning models to predict the blood pressure from the extracted features. In this work, an approach that does not need calibration or manual feature extraction is proposed using CNNs and LSTMs for BP prediction.

Glioma Prognosis: Segmentation of the Tumor and Survival Prediction using Shape, Geometric and Clinical Information
Mobarakol Islam, V Jeya Maria Jose, Hongliang Ren

Segmentation of brain tumor from magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is performed using a convolutional neural network (CNN) with hypercolumn technique. Also, a variety of features are extracted from the segmented tumor to predict the overall survival in terms of number of days for each patient. This work was done as a part of BraTS challenge 2018 conducted by University of Pennyslvania.

Ischemic Stroke Lesion Segmentation Using Adversarial Learning
Mobarakol Islam Rajiv V V Jeya Maria Jose, Hongliang Ren

A segmentation model with adversarial learning for ischemic lesion segmentation. U-Net with skip connection and dropout is adopted as segmentation baseline network and a fully connected network (FCN) is used as discriminator network. Three modalities (CT, DPWI, CBF) of acute computed tomography (CT) perfusion data provided in ISLES 2018 (Ischemic Stroke Lesion Segmentation) for ischemic lesion segmentation are used to train the net.

Adversarial Learning of Dilated Resnet with Pyramid Pooling for Nuclei Segmentation (ongoing)
Mobarakol Islam, V Jeya Maria Jose, Hongliang Ren

A novel adversarial segmentation model for nuclear segmentation inspired from generative adversarial networks is proposed. Segmentation of nuclei from microscopic images of tumor cells from affected tissues is of a great use in computational pathology. The dataset used was published by the MoNuSeg Challenge at MICCAI 2018.
